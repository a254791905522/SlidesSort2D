This page explains how Slide Sort 2D handles information. It is written for players and caregivers in plain English. The game is distributed on the Apple App Store; Apple’s own systems also apply when you download or update the app.

01

Play without signing in

Slide Sort 2D does not require you to create a separate in-game account. Progress, coins, daily challenge state, and similar gameplay data can be stored locally on your device so you can pick up where you left off.

02

What stays on your iPhone or iPad

Level completion, booster usage, mode preferences, and other session information typically remain inside the app’s sandbox. Removing the app or clearing its data through iOS settings deletes that local storage.

03

App Store privacy summary

According to the App Store “App Privacy” section for Slide Sort 2D, the developer does not collect data from you through the app as Apple defines collection. If that label changes after an update, the listing on the App Store is the authoritative source Apple displays.

Apple separately processes purchases, downloads, device diagnostics, and account-level settings under Apple’s privacy policy—not under this game’s control.

04

Advertising

The App Store indicates that the app may contain advertising. Ad networks (when shown) operate under their own policies. You can review iOS privacy settings and Screen Time controls to manage tracking and content where Apple provides options.

05

Younger players

The game is rated 4+. If children use a shared family device, use Apple’s parental controls for installs, in-app purchases, and screen time rather than relying on this page alone.

06

Updates to this overview

If a future version adds online accounts, analytics, or other features that change how data is handled, this page will be updated to reflect that behavior. The installed build and the App Store privacy label should always be checked first.

Your choices

Uninstalling Slide Sort 2D removes its local data from the device. For Apple ID privacy, use Settings → Privacy & Security on your device.

Scope

This overview is a readable summary, not legal advice. It does not replace mandatory disclosures in your region or Apple’s App Store terms.

If anything here conflicts with the installed app or the App Store “App Privacy” section, rely on the store listing and on-device behavior first, then treat this page as a companion explanation.
